Psalms for When You Are Afraid

May 12, 2026 · Fear & Anxiety

Fear is a shadow that visits every life. It shows up at night when the house is quiet, in the doctor's waiting room, before an important decision, or when the news feels heavier than usual.

The Book of Psalms was written by people who knew fear. David ran for his life. The psalmists wept through exile. They felt abandoned, threatened, and overwhelmed. And they left us a gift — words to pray when we don't have our own.

Here are psalms to turn to when fear knocks at your door.

Psalm 23 — The Lord Is My Shepherd

Perhaps the most famous chapter in the Bible. It reorients your entire perspective — you are not alone, you are led, provided for, and pursued by goodness.

“Even though I walk through the darkest valley, I will fear no evil, for you are with me.”Psalm 23:4

Psalm 27 — The Lord Is My Light

When fear feels like darkness, this psalm declares God is light. It's a declaration of confidence in the face of opposition.

“The Lord is my light and my salvation — whom shall I fear?”Psalm 27:1

Psalm 34 — Taste and See

David wrote this after a season of extreme danger. It's a testimony — “I sought the Lord, and he answered me.”

“I sought the Lord, and he answered me; he delivered me from all my fears.”Psalm 34:4

Psalm 91 — Under His Wings

This is the psalm of divine protection. It paints a picture of safety so complete that you can rest even as danger surrounds you.

“He will cover you with his feathers, and under his wings you will find refuge.”Psalm 91:4

Psalm 121 — Where Does My Help Come From?

A pilgrimage psalm for those who need to remember that help comes from above. It's a reminder that God never sleeps, never tires, and never forgets you.

“My help comes from the Lord, the Maker of heaven and earth.”Psalm 121:2

How to use these psalms: Read them aloud. Pray them back to God. Write one on a note and keep it where you can see it. Let the ancient words become your words.
